ABOUT ME

Some pictures of me

Hello! If you find yourself here, I'm guessing you're looking to learn a bit more about me. As I'm sure you've seen, my name is Tyler Nielson. I am a senior at Kansas State University, majoring in Software Engineering. I'm only 21 credit hours away from graduation, which I find is rather terrifying to think about.

Abridged History: Birth - 11 Years

To go way back in time, I was born in Logan, Utah to two loving parents. I spent only a year there before we packed up and moved to Laramie, Wyoming so my dad could finish his education. During my time there, I attended preschool. Yup. That's about all I remember from my time there. After spending ~four years in Laramie, we shipped off to Bettendorf, Iowa, where I spent the next seven years attending elementary and, for one year, middle school. This is really where I begin to remember things about my past. I remember having two really good friends, Ryan Gist and Morgan Barker. They were the two primary friends I spent time with in the seven years I spent in Iowa. During those seven years, I also was gifted the pleasure of two younger siblings, a brother and a sister born two years apart. Life wasn't so lonely after that as I was no longer an only child, but it was now a lot more hectic and there was a LOT of fighting. This fighting, of course, came primarily from the two young ones as they grew older, who couldn't seem to be in the same room without waging war on each other.

It was also in Iowa where I began to take an interest in engineering. I was a small kid with big plans, expecting to go to school for Environmental Engineering, just like my dad. During this phase, my computer skills quietly improved without me realizing it. I had always had a knack for technology and I should've just chosen to major in a technology field from the beginning, but I digress.

Abridged History: 12 Years - 20 Years

After seven years, the family once again packed up and moved to where they currently reside, in Shawnee Kansas. As you can imagine, moving when I was 11 years old was basically the end of the world. "I'll lose all of my friends!" "I don't want to leave, I like it here!" were among the phrases I spewed when my parents broke the news to me. Little did I know that the move here would be one of the better things that happens in my life.

We moved to Kansas shortly before my 12th birthday. My dad had already been down here working at his new job, so we had a house that was ready for us when we got here. "Ready" as in, we had one, sans furniture. The movers followed us down and we had our furniture from Iowa here in a matter of a few days. Let the unpacking begin! (Speaking of, I'm 90% sure there's still boxes that are packed away in the basement...)
